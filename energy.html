Work

When an object is displaced due to a force, it is said that work is being done on that object. In order for the force to qualify as work, the object must be displaced by the force. The equation for work is
W=FrCosΘ,
which means work is equal to the force multiplied by the displacement times the angle between force and displacement. This concept of work relates to energy because work is also defined as
W=ΔE,
where ΔE is the change in energy. In this way, we can relate forces and energy together through substitution, giving us the newly derived formula
FrCosΘ=ΔE

 

Energy all around us

Law of Conservation of Energy- Energy is neither created nor destroyed, it merely changes forms, such as: heat,light,motion, etc... Therefore the total energy of a system is always constant. The unit of measure of energy is joules.

Kinetic Energy- This is the energy of motion, every moving object has kinetic energy. The equation for kinetic energy is KE=1/2 * m * v2. This equation states that kinetic energy is proportional to its speed squared. If speed is doubled -- kinetic energy is quadrupled. By using the relationship between kinetic energy and mass and volume, 1 joule= 1 kg * m2/s2 can be derived. Kinetic energy is a scalar quantity, which means it does not have a direction.

Potential Energy-This is the energy stored in an object. Potential energy changes depending on the position of the object. There are two types of potential energy, Gravitational potential energy and Elastic potential energy.

Gravitational Potential Energy-Gravitational potential energy is stored depending on the object's height. It is created by the object's gravitaional attraction to the Earth.The magnitude of this energy is dependent on the mass of the object and its height. The formula for PEgrav=m * g * h ; therefore, when an object is on the ground it is considered to have no potential energy, meaning the higher the object is, the more mass it has, or the stronger the gravity results in an increase in potentil energy.

Elastic Potential Energy-Elastic Potential energy is stored in elastic materials that stretch or compress (rubber bands, bungee cords, trampolines, springs, etc...), where the longer the material is the more elastic potential energy can be stored. The formula for the potential energy of springs is
PEspring=1/2 * k * x2 where k is the spring constant and x is the amount stretched or compressed.

Mechanical Energy- The energy to do work. Mechanical energy is composed of kinetic and potential energy. Anything that has potential or kinetic energy has mechanical energy. An object that possesses mechanical has the ability to displace other objects. Since mechanical energy is composed of kinetic and potential energy, the total mechanical energy can be calculated by
MEtotal=PE + KE which can be translated to MEtotal=(m*g*h) + (1/2 *m * v2).



The video above helps visualize the principle of Conservation of Energy. A roller coaster track with a roller coaster cart can be thought of as one complete system. The net energy in this system will stay the same, but each form of energy will change throughout the ride. When the roller coaster gets to higher points, it has plentiful potential energy, so there is less kinetic energy. As you already know, kinetic energy is the energy of motion, and if there is less kinetic energy there is less motion. This explains why you feel the suspense as you slowly go upward to the top on a roller coaster and immediately fall below with great speed. As your height changes throughout the ride, your potential and kinetic energies fluctuate and balance each other out.
