Magnetic Forces

Magnetic Forces are the forces that result from the interactions of the electrical charges in iron. This magnetic force extends its influence on other iron objects.Newton's key rule for motion in physics. If its not already moving, it won't suddenly start moving until some outside force acts on it. In this experiment, there just isn't enough force acting on the dishes for them to be jerked off the table if done properly.

Applying Magnetic Forces

Materials

  • Two Magnets
  • Table
  • Iron filing

Procedure
To perform this experiment, take the magnets and try to push them together with the same poles i.e. North Pole to North Pole. How far are they apart until you feel the "push"? Now lay one on a flat surface and hover the second one over it. Try this numerous times. Does the distance of the "pull" change? Now place both magnets on a flat smooth surface with poles facing each other and sprinkle iron filings around the magnets. What happens?

Observation
While pushing them together, notice how the distance at which the magnets begin to repel each other remains constant. The same can be said about the attraction of the magnets. Finally, notice how the iron filings will oddly rearrange themselves to point upward.

Explanation
TThe distance where the attractive/repulsive forces between the magnets are effective is constant because the magnetic fields of the magnets stay intact unless the magnets are seriously altered i.e. broken, heated, or cooled. It is also the force of the magnetic field influencing that of another, making the filings stand up in an odd manner.
