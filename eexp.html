Sound Waves

Sound is a form of energy that travels in waves. These waves send vibrations through a medium, in other words, they shake the molecules of whatever matter they travel through. That is why this experiment works, the sound energy from the speakers is so powerful that the air molecules vibrate enough for us to notice them vinrating the cup of water. Note that this same vibration is experienced by your ear drums everyday, allowing you to hear.

Applying Sound Energy

Materials

  • Speakers
  • Table
  • Cup of Water

Procedure
To perform this experiment simply increase the volume of the speakers and place the cup of water next to it. Observe how the volume of the speaker affects the water; also try changing the distance of the cup from the speaker. Did the distance have an effect on the water?

Observation
The water should vibrate more when the volume is higher and it should vibrate less when the cup is farther away. The sound energy is transformed from electric energy via the electric socket. The electric energy could be created by magnets, chemical energy (burning coal etc…), hydroelectric, solar, wind, etc…

Explanation
The water vibrates more when the volume is high because sound energy is higher, and since sound is a wave its energy diminishes with distance. The reason the water moves is because sound is a mechanical wave, meaning it needs a medium to travel through. The medium is usually air, but sound can also travel through solid and liquid mediums.
