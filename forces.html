What's in aForce?



Forces

A force is any influence tending to change the motion of a body or produce motion or stress in a stationary object. It's also the "external force" Newton's First Law states is needed to change the movement of an object. Think of it as simply a push or a pull in any direction, or possibly in every direction. The ideal way to visualize it is to simply imagine dropping a rock of a high platform. The rock gets faster as Earth continually pulls on it. Since Earth keeps pulling, the velocity of the rock steadily increases. Since forces influence motion, there is a simple formula that relates and objects motion to the force it experiences:


F = ma

Quite simply, the net force acting on an object is equal to the product of its mass and acceleration. We can use this formula to evaluate the magnitude of forces, ranging from the force of gravity on a basketball to the force that propells a rocket into outer space. It's a pretty cool formula.

Forces they're everywhere

Gravitational Force-The force of attraction between all masses in the universe. This force is defined as the weight of an object. It is what pulls everything on Earth towards the Earth's center. Gravitational force is proportional to the product of the two masses and invesly propotional to the square of the distance between them.

Normal Force-Normal force is explained by Newron's third law of motion,for every action there is an opposite and equal reaction. Normal force is perpendicular to the surface of contact and can usually balance out with the weight of an object(unless if the object is too heavy of course). For example a text book on a desk is being pulled towards Earth through Gravity and pushed back by the desk.

Friction Force-The force of friction is created by the contact of two serfaces being pressed closely together, causing attractive forces from different surfaces; therefore, friction depends on the nature of the surfaces and the degree they are pressed together. Friction usually opposes the direction the object moves in. There are two types of friction-static and sliding.

Air Resistance-This force is caused by objects moving through air, and is usually ignored due to negligible effects and difficulty calculating. This force is most noticable on objects that travel at high speeds or objects with large surface area.

Tension-The force that effects strings,ropes,cables, or wire when it is pulled tightly by forces acting on opposite ends. The force moves along the wire and pulls equally on the object on the opposite end of the wire.

Spring Force-This force is exerted by a compressed or stretched spring upon any object attached to it. Any object that stretches or compresses a spring will be acted upon by this force that tries to restore the spring to equilibrium. For most springs the force is directly proportional to the number of coils(Hooke's Law).
